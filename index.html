Professional meeting minutes in seconds!

Getting Started

Let's create some minutes! Minutes.nz allows you to create a set of formal meeting minutes quickly and easily.

You can use Minutes.nz after your meeting to create a set of minutes, or you can use it while in the meeting.

Using Minutes.nz in your meeting makes it even easier by providing you with guidance to help run the meeting.

Organisation Name

Enter the full name of your organisation.

Meeting date

Enter the date that you are meeting.

Meeting location

Enter the name of the place where you are meeting.

Attendees

List the names of the attendees. Separate each name by a comma.

Apologies

Are there any apologies?

List the names of group members that cannot attend this meeting. Separate each name by a comma.

Previous Meeting

We record the date of the previous meeting because...

Are the Minutes of the Previous Meeting a True and Correct Record?

Who would like to move that we accept that the minutes of the previous meeting are an accurate record?

Matters Arising From Previous Minutes

Does anyone have any further comments regarding what was discussed at the last meeting?

Any new issues, or issues of substance from the previous meeting should be included as an agenda item and discussed later in the meeting.

If there is no further discussion about the last meeting, leave this blank.

Correspondence

Has anybody received any information or correspondence since the last meeting?

Financial Report

Is there a Financial Report being presented today?

Other Reports

Are there any other reports being presented today?

For example your President or Chairperson may be presenting a report.

Agenda Items

Give each agenda item a brief title and description.

For each agenda item, make notes as to what is discussed. If any decisions are made where you wish to formally record, or your rules state that you should do so, record these decisions in a motion.

Motions

If any decisions are made where you wish to formally record, if your rules state that you should do so, record these decisions in a motion:

Would someone like to move this motion?

Motions usually start with "That....." for example "That the Club make an application to the Wonderful Charity for a donation of funds to assist with the purchase a gyrocopter"

Do we have a seconder to this motion?

Is everyone in favour?

Did everyone agree? If not you may have to take a vote, amend the motion or make a note as to why it was not passed. Don't forget to refer to the rules of your organisation, as you may have some specific rules which have to be followed.
